Pride march
26th July 2025, 12:00-13:00
starting in Sydney Gardens
Pride March through the city, pride is a protest!
Pride is a protest. Marches are an important part of pride, they provide an opportunity for the LGBTQ+ community to come together to further our rights, and try to create a more tolerant and accepting city.
Accessibility
Please see the carousel below for photographs of the whole route. Key information:
  • Step free
  • Some slopes and uneven paving
  • Toilets on the route can be found via the Toilet Finder app and Great British Public Toilet Map

How to March

We will be assembling the parade in Sydney Gardens at 11:45 to prepare for the march, which is due to start at 12:00 sharp. Below, there is a map of the march, and a photo guide to the route. The march will take the following route:
  • Sydney Place
  • Great Pulteney Street
  • Argyle Street
  • Pulteney Bridge
  • A3039
  • Cheap Street
  • Union Street
  • Upper Borough Walls
  • A3039
  • Pulteney Bridge
  • Argyle Street
  • Great Pulteney Street
  • William Street
It is important if you are taking part in the march that you pay attention to all stewards and marshalls and follow their instructions. You must agree to the terms and conditions at the bottom of this page to take part in our march, to ensure we meet our responsibilities in keeping people safe and keeping the march to schedule on the day.
We have identified a few toilets along the route: Sydney Gardens, Parade Gardens, Bath Abbey, Waitrose, Henrietta Park. Please be aware the march will not be able to wait for you, but do feel free to meet your needs and rejoin us later when you're ready.
Map of the march route
Walk down Great Pulteney Street towards the Laura Place Fountain
Walk down Argyle Street, over Pulteney Bridge
Turn left at the traffic lights opposite Guildhall
Walk down Grand Parade (past Pulteney Weir)
Turn right, between Parade Gardens and The Empire Hotel
Turn right around Orange Grove, past the taxi rank at the back of the Abbey
Turn left, around the Abbey and towards the city centre shops
Walk down Cheap Street until you see the Costa on your left, where Cheap Street meets Stall Street and Union Street
Walk up Union Street until it meets the Upper Borough Walls (uphill, take care and pace yourselves as needed)
Stop at the top of Union Street, ready to turn right
Turn right onto Upper Borough Walls
Walk all the way down Upper Borough Walls
Be wary of uneven paving in this area
Cross the road and head down Bridge Street
This brings us back in a loop to Pulteney Bridge and Argyle Street
Walk back down Argyle Street towards the Laura Place Fountain
Walk past the Laura Place Fountain and back onto Great Pulteney Street
